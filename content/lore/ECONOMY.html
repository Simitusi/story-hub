1. The Outside World Economy (Inter-faction, wasteland level)

This is the part that keeps the world from collapsing into isolated bubbles.

Universal value: Drugs

Drugs are the closest thing to real money outside faction borders.

Why they work:

  • universal demand

  • portable, divisible

  • testable quality

  • value tied to survival, not ideology

  • addiction guarantees circulation

Different drugs have different value:

  • stabilizers > enhancers > recreational

  • clean supply > cut junk

  • known origin > mystery mix

Drugs are used for:

  • inter-faction trade

  • bribery

  • emergency leverage

  • paying smugglers, mercs, informants

  • hoarding long-term wealth

They are not used for daily life inside strong factions, because that would destabilize control.

Supplementary external trade

Alongside drugs:

  • ammo

  • fuel

  • tools

  • information

  • slaves (as capital, not currency)

Barter still exists, but drugs smooth large transactions and long routes

2. Marauder Internal Economy (Nyx’s system)

Inside Marauder territory, drugs are deliberately sidelined.

Currency: Marks

Marks are not money in the abstract sense.
They are allowances and clearance.

Marks are:

  • only usable inside Marauder territory

  • worth nothing outside

  • issued by Nyx

  • rotated monthly

  • different every month (old marks expire)

They buy:

  • food

  • rent / housing

  • market access

  • basic internal services

They do not represent wealth.
They represent recognized existence for this month.

3. Distribution and hierarchy

Subgang leaders

  • Each subgang leader receives a fixed monthly allotment of marks from Nyx

  • That allotment is calculated exactly for the number of members Nyx officially recognizes

  • Leaders distribute marks to their crew

Important:

  • There is no upward complaint channel

  • If people don’t eat or can’t pay rent, it is the leader’s fault

  • Nyx is never blamed, structurally or socially

Anyone unregistered:

  • slaves

  • captives

  • guests

  • traders staying overnight

are the subgang’s responsibility, not Nyx’s.

4. Production, competition, and pressure

No quotas. Only competition.

Nyx does not set production quotas.

Instead:

  • subgangs compete through productivity

  • surplus sent to the center is rewarded

  • underproduction is punished indirectly

If a subgang produces surplus:

  • Nyx grants extra marks for that month

  • this feels like a reward, not a promise

  • it raises expectations for everyone else

If a subgang underproduces:

  • they receive fewer marks next month

  • or the leader must compensate through:

    • drugs

    • ammo

    • raids

    • selling assets

    • selling people

Failure is framed as leadership incompetence, not systemic fault.

5. Inflation as punishment

The center (Nyx) relies on supply taxes from subgangs.

If total supply drops:

  • internal conversion rates worsen

  • marks convert into fewer goods

  • everyone suffers

Crucially:

  • inflation is blamed on underproductive subgangs

  • anger turns sideways and downward

  • Nyx intervenes only after unrest builds

Nyx never causes shortages.
The system does.

6. Mark circulation and settlement

During the month

  • workers, cooks, guards, traders, service providers are paid in marks

  • marks circulate internally

  • hoarding is pointless because they expire

End-of-month settlement

Near the end of the cycle:

  • marks can be converted at official points into:

    • drugs

    • ammo

    • fuel

    • supplies

Conversion rates:

  • change month to month

  • depend on overall productivity

  • discourage early conversion

Because of this:

  • rushing to convert marks is irrational

  • waiting is safer

  • marks remain liquid internally until settlement

Not everyone wants drugs:

  • some want ammo

  • some want tools

  • some want protection credit

This prevents drugs from becoming internal daily currency.


7. Hoarding logic

You cannot hoard marks:

  • they expire

You can hoard:

  • drugs

  • ammo

  • tools

  • supplies

Which creates a clean split:

  • marks = short-term survival

  • drugs = long-term power

8. Hellhound’s role in this economy

Hellhound is uniquely dangerous to herself in this system.

As a subgang leader:

  • she is fully responsible for her crew’s survival

  • failure harms everyone

  • success raises expectations

Her response:

  • solo raids to cover deficits

  • personal risk to stabilize numbers

  • absorbing bad months with her own body

This:

  • keeps her gang competitive

  • hides systemic strain from Nyx

  • makes the system appear stable

  • burns her out quietly

Hellhound becomes a shock absorber for Nyx’s economy.

9. What Nyx actually controls

Nyx does not control:

  • individual effort

  • daily transactions

  • personal survival choices

Nyx controls:

  • recognition

  • allowance

  • settlement timing

  • conversion rates

  • intervention thresholds

She rules through access, delay, and blame, not direct force.

Final synthesis

Your economy is:

  • Externally: drug-based, brutal, universal

  • Internally: allowance-based, competitive, blame-driven

  • Structurally: stable through rivalry, not trust

  • Thematically: addiction turned into economics

Marks keep people alive.
Drugs keep people powerful.
Leaders keep people compliant.
Nyx keeps all of it deniable.
