terms-and-labels

@PYRA

Pyradrenaline Response Agonist PR-A4
Hardline combat stim built to hijack fight-or-flight and weld the throttle open. In a regular wastelander, one dose is already enough to make them unstable, hyper-aggressive and reckless; two usually means system collapse or psychotic break. It kills pain, spikes strength and sensory drive, and drags buried emotions to the surface in one violent rush.

A tiny minority of people with freakishly high neurostimulant tolerance can stack doses into distinct tiers of effect instead of just dying; those are the ones everyone tells horror stories about when they say “she’s on Pyra again.”

@RZ

Razine Hydrochloride
Nerve-scrambler “calm.” Takes the edge off panic and rage without making you sleepy; long-term users get emotional flatline and hand tremors.

@HUSH

Hypothalamic Suppression Serum (HSS-3)
Turns down fear, startle reflex, and guilt. People on Hush can walk through gunfire with a blank face; when it wears off, nightmares hit like a truck.

@Gravewater

Post-necrotic Cellular Buffer Solvent G-17
Cheap injectable that lets people run on zero sleep for a day or two by cannibalizing their own reserves. After a binge, users crash like corpses, hence the name.

@AX-7

Adrenergic Xenostimulant Compound 7
Classic combat stim: faster reflexes, boosted strength, pain barely registers. Overuse shreds the cardiovascular system; vein blowouts are a known side effect.

@Blue Wire

Neuroconductive Modulator NEU-Δ7
Focus drug popular with techs and snipers; sharpens fine motor control, slows subjective time a bit. Users stare too long, forget they have a body until it cramps.

@Moth/M.O.T.H.

Mono-Oxidative Thalamic Hallucinogen
Soft hallucinations, drifting lights, quiet voices; often used in dens to “float” above reality. Long-term users get persistent visual snow and afterimages.

@Glass

Crystalline Cortical Agonist CCA-3
Hyper-clarity high: everything feels sharp, clean, solvable. Then comes the crash: indecision, intrusive thoughts, paralysis. Addictive as hell among planners and tacticians.

@Halo

Halophrenyl Nocturate
Sleep isn’t guaranteed in the wastes, so people fake it chemically. Halo knocks you out fast and deep, but REM is chaotic; many users wake up more unstable than before.

@FOG

Fronto-Occipital GABA Enhancer
Anti-flashback drug. Blurs memory formation and emotional tagging; soldiers, raiders, and slaves use it to erase specific nights. Overuse makes people permanently “distant.”

@SNAP

Synaptic Norepinephrine Acceleration Protocol (SN-αP)
Short, vicious burst stim. Fifteen to thirty seconds of speed and violence, then shaking and nausea. Street brawlers love it because it turns the opening move into a kill-shot.

@Echo dust

Acoustic Sensory Potentiator ASP-9 (Microgranular)
Snorted powder that jacks up hearing and vibration sense; scouts swear by it. Side effects: tinnitus, sensory overload, paranoia about sounds no one else hears.
